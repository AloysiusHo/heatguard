Process

From skin to screen in seconds

A slim patch worn on the wrist or forearm captures continuous skin temperature. No buttons. No charging mid-shift.

01 — SENSE
🩹
Wearable Patch
A flex-circuit sensor worn on the inner wrist logs skin temperature every 10 seconds throughout the shift.
→
02 — TRANSMIT
📡
BLE + Site Gateway
Data hops via Bluetooth Low Energy to a site gateway. No cell data needed — works inside shielded structures.
→
03 — ANALYSE
🧠
Threshold Engine
Cloud logic cross-references ambient temperature, humidity, and worker history to compute personalised risk scores.
→
04 — ALERT
📲
Manager Notification
Push alerts reach the PM's phone and dashboard, naming the worker, zone, and suggested action — immediately.

Platform

Built for the site office, not the lab

📍
Zone-level location
Alerts include the zone and level the worker was last seen — no GPS needed, just site beacon anchors every 20 m.
📊
Shift trend history
Review how temperatures climbed through the day. Spot the 2 PM spike and adjust break schedules before the next shift.
🔔
Escalating alerts
A watch nudge at 37.5°C. A push alert at 38°C. A call and site siren at 38.5°C. Every level has a defined response protocol.
🌡️
Ambient correlation
Pairs skin data with a site weather station — accounting for humidity and radiant heat, not just air temperature.
📋
Compliance export
One-click PDF reports per shift, ready for MOM submission. Timestamped, worker-anonymised by default.
♻️
Resource reallocation
Cross-site heat index dashboard lets you move cooling assets, water stations, and crews to where they're needed most.
Alert Protocol

Three levels. Clear actions. No ambiguity.

Every threshold maps directly to a response — so your supervisors never have to decide what to do next.

≤37.4°
Normal
Worker is within safe operating range. No intervention needed. Dashboard shows green.
→ Monitor passively
37.5–38°
Caution
Elevated skin temp detected. PM receives a push notification. Worker should hydrate and move to shade.
→ Notify PM · Schedule break
>38°
Danger
Potential heat stress. Loud siren on site. Supervisor calls worker. First aid on standby. Work in zone suspended.
→ Evacuate zone · Medical check
